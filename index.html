Inclusion criteria

What counts as a competitor

Three hard criteria from Operating Model v3.0. A company must pass all three to be in the comparison table.

✓1

Buys SMB operators

Acquires going-concern operating businesses in a vertical and owns them. NOT a SaaS/agents vendor selling software TO industries.

✓2

Integrates AI INSIDE the acquired companies

Embeds proprietary AI tooling layer inside each portco to drive productivity uplift. NOT a consulting/FDE shop that delivers AI services to external customers.

✓3

Three-entity architecture (or operational analogue)

Either explicit Fund + AI-Integration Co + HoldCo split, or a single operating holdco running AI-integrated rollup. NOT a classical software holdco without integrated AI thesis, NOT a VC fund.

Path Forward

По какому пути идти — 8 strategic moves

The narrowing window. Our two defensible edges (productivity gate + vertical SMB focus) have a 12–24 month half-life. By Q1 2027, expect 2–3 more dedicated closed-end LP vehicles announcing into the same niche, AI-services firms raising follow-ons with acquisition vehicles attached, and Thrive launching vertical-specific HoldCos.
01

Accelerate HomeCare pilot to public win before Q1 2027

HomeCare is our cleanest whitespace — no major AI-rollup competitor identified. YCare × Alan Gobbs needs documented ≥1.5× uplift case study + first HoldCo acquisition + verifiable EBITDA uplift inside 6-9 months.

02

Drop the table-stakes tagline

"Build AI first, then acquire" is now industry-standard. Reposition around the two genuinely defensible mechanisms: ≥1.5× productivity gate before deploying acquisition capital (no competitor uses this) and sub-$10M EBITDA SMB focus (GC, Thrive, and Shield all target $50M+ EBITDA or 30+ portcos).

03

Make the productivity gate the headline

The ≥1.5× uplift gate is our ONLY truly unique mechanism. Document methodology publicly: how we measure, what counts, what doesn't. Use in every LP conversation as risk-reduction story.

04

Anthropic / Meta / xAI strategic partnership

OpenAI took equity in Thrive (Dec 2025) → embeds engineers at zero capital cost. If every Thrive portco gets OpenAI eng for free, our Intwin Delivery cost advantage flips at scale. Need analogous partnership — Anthropic logical, xAI bigger swing.

05

Sub-segment AWAY from Long Lake in PM

Sergey × PM pilot needs explicit differentiation: commercial PM (vs Long Lake residential HOA), distinct geography, or different operator archetype (Config B = operator-with-software vs Long Lake M&A-by-anchor).

06

VC funds = LP candidates, NOT competitors

Khosla, Lightspeed, Slow Ventures, 8VC, Elad Gil — initial draft mis-classified them as "AI-first PE funds." They are VCs investing in stand-alone AI startups, not roll-up operators. Treat as capital ecosystem: potential LPs, co-investors, or sourcing partners. Vinod Khosla's "won't do alone" remains open door for anchor LP.

07

Deferred-payment acquisitions tied to EBITDA = no. Upfront cash + earnout on organic-growth metrics. Build for 5%+ Fed funds env. Consider Permanent Equity's 0-mgmt-fee + carry-on-cash for HoldCo carry economics.

Cautionary cases

Thrasio + OpenStore — 5 failure modes for HoldCo design

Thrasio raised $3B+ then Ch.11 Feb 2024 ($10B peak val); OpenStore $970M → ~$50M. Both Amazon FBA aggregators. Lessons directly applicable to v3.0 HoldCo structure:

  1. Deferred-payment acquisitions = earnings-recursion riskThrasio paid sellers in 2-3 year tranches contingent on portco performance. When COVID e-comm normalized, they couldn't pay. Use upfront cash + earnout on organic-growth metrics.
  2. No operational moat = no roll-upAmazon FBA brands had no defensible tech. Each independently buyable. Our vertical AI built inside operator IS our moat — structural reason Bobroff differs.
  3. Capital availability ≠ thesis validationEasy money 2020-21 created aggregator boom; rate hikes killed it. Build for 5%+ Fed funds env from day one.
  4. Multiple compression works ONLY with operational upliftThrasio paid 4-5× EBITDA, got nothing structural in return. Our 5×→11-16× insurance arbitrage needs AI margin uplift backing the multiple expansion.
  5. Single-platform concentration = SPOFBobroff's multi-vertical HoldCo (HomeCare, MSP, Accounting, P&C, PM) structurally more diversified than any Amazon aggregator.
Cross-cutting trends

Patterns across the 5 Tier-1 competitors

Six structural patterns observed across GC Creation, Thrive Holdings, Long Lake, Shield Tech, and Titan — and what they imply for Bobroff Cap's positioning.

● Pattern 1 — Capital
Permanent capital is the new structural moat

Thrive Holdings + Long Lake both run perpetual capital. GC Creation explicitly considering Fund XIII with permanent vehicle. Reason: 10-yr LP fund mechanics force-sell HoldCos at year 8, destroying compound value. Our gap: Bobroff's $50M Delaware LP is 10-yr by design.

Counter-move: Hybrid structure — Fund I as LP capital, but Intwin Delivery (AI-Integration Co) as evergreen GP-owned tooling entity that survives any fund.

● Pattern 2 — Talent
Palantir Mafia owns the talent layer

Shield CEO Jim Siders (13yr Palantir CIO), Mayank Jain (ex-Palantir product), Long Lake hires from Palantir/Ramp/Robinhood, Thrive Holdings team majority ex-Ramp + Palantir. Bob McGrew (ex-Palantir #2 eng + ex-OpenAI CRO) connects the mafias across the space.

Counter-move: Hire 1-2 ex-Palantir FDE at 18-36 month tenure mark — sweet spot before Shield IPO equity vests them.

● Pattern 3 — AI partnership
OpenAI = vertical embed; Anthropic/Google = open

OpenAI took equity in Thrive Holdings (Dec 2025), embedding researchers/PMs/engineers inside Crete + Shield + future verticals. GC Creation uses Mistral as default model across portcos. Open lanes: Anthropic enterprise (no equivalent partnership), Meta, xAI.

Counter-move: Lock Anthropic enterprise relationship + recruit senior Anthropic FDE leader as Operator-in-Residence (mirror James Dyett at Thrive).

● Pattern 4 — Deal structure
60-90% acquisition with seller rollover = standard

Shield: 60-90% with 10-40% rollover. Long Lake: founder/CEO retention universal. Crete: partner firms retain brand + local autonomy. Bhargava (GC): "30% seller rollover is key differentiator vs PE." All keep founders 12-24mo earnouts.

Counter-move: Adopt 60-90% + 10-40% rollover as Bobroff HoldCo standard. Cite Shield's published 9-of-9 founder retention as proof.

● Pattern 5 — Tech architecture
Captive AI tooling, not SaaS-licensed

Sentinel + Spectre (Shield) = platform-owned, NOT available to non-Shield MSPs. Nexus AI (Long Lake) = 80% horizontal/20% vertical, model-agnostic. Titan's "Augmented AI" platform = proprietary, deployed inside acquired MSPs. Pattern: Own the tooling layer, lease the models.

Counter-move: Intwin Delivery must build proprietary tooling layer (not just orchestrate frontier models). This is the moat that compounds.

● Pattern 6 — Industry capture
Vertical-specialization beats horizontal scale

Shield's strongest = vertical-specialized: SK Tech dental (400 practices), NetAscendant Permian oil&gas, Option One biotech/finance. Long Lake = HOA-only. Crete = accounting-only. Titan = FS-MSP only. Vertical-niche beats horizontal scale in early platform years.

Counter-move: Stay vertical-specialized in HomeCare, P&C tier-2, Accounting tier-2. Cite Shield Tech as proof that vertical-niche beats horizontal scale in early platform years.

The "Bobroff Cap defensible position" synthesis

After mapping all 7 — these are the only positions where Bobroff Cap can credibly differentiate:

  1. Geographic moat (within US): Long Lake's Sun Belt residential HOA focus leaves Northeast metro commercial PM open; Titan/RFA concentrated in NYC/Boston/London FS-MSP leaves US secondary metros (Atlanta, Phoenix, Denver, Minneapolis) open; Shield's vertical-specialized MSP focus leaves regional generalist MSPs in Midwest/South open
  2. Sub-segment moat: Sub-$10M EBITDA tuck-ins where $2B Thrive, $1.5B GC Creation, $200M Shield cannot deploy time/attention
  3. Vertical whitespace: HomeCare (no major direct comp identified), P&C insurance tier-2 (no AI rollup), faith-based / municipal / agricultural IT (Shield gaps)
  4. Process moat: ≥1.5× productivity gate before acquisition — none of 7 competitors run this; Shield's 60% automation + 50% TTR validates 1.45× empirically achievable
  5. Capital structure: Three-entity Fund + IntCo + HoldCo with productivity gate = cleaner LP story than GC's Long Lake 85% concentration or Thrive's "circular dealmaking" (TechCrunch)
  6. Augmentation framing: Industry-standard "AI replaces work" critique = reputational vulnerability for incumbents. Bobroff position as augmentation-first throughout (LP-aligned, post-2025 backlash safe)
